CRITICAL CAVEATS FOR USE IN BC DECKS / SALES PITCHES
================================================================
1. EUROPE numbers are 3DS success rate (Ravelin 2026), not pure issuer auth rate.
   Pure auth rate pre-3DS typically runs 5-10pp higher (88-94%).

2. MEXICO 69% is e-commerce. Card-present is ~97%. Always specify channel.

3. BRAZIL 80% is DOMESTIC only. Cross-border international cards drop to ~30%
   (biggest gap globally). Use this gap as a sales lever.

4. APAC EMERGING (China, Indonesia, Philippines, Vietnam, Thailand): cards are
   5-14% of total payments. The 90% regional average is misleading here — the
   business-relevant metric is wallet success rate, not card auth rate.

5. MEA average 79% blends Gulf (typically 80-90%) with sub-Saharan Africa
   (typically 60-75%). If pitching specifically to GCC or SSA, segment.

6. Documented Network Token uplift (cite freely):
   - LATAM average:        +7pp (EBANX)
   - Colombia specifically: +10pp (EBANX)
   - Peru SaaS:             +8pp (EBANX)
   - KSA Mada local-routing:+8-12pp (Apaya)
   - MENA tokenized:        +3-6pp (Checkout x Mastercard May 2026)
   - Fraud-related declines: -86% (EBANX)

7. Smart Routing case study: inDrive achieved 90% auth rate via Yuno Smart
   Routing across LATAM (case study cleared for citation).

8. Local acquiring gap (cite freely):
   - Brazil cross-border: 30% offshore -> 60%+ with local acquiring (Worldline)
   - LATAM generally:     20-45% offshore -> 60-80% local (Worldline)
   - MENA single-PSP:     loses 12-20% of legitimate transactions (Apaya)
